04 / about

Hey, I'm Luke!

Information Science and Technology Student who gravitates towards turning "I wonder if I could build that" into a project at 11pm. I gravitate toward problems that sit at intersections. Whether it is the intersection of business needs, or intersections of machines and data.

I've spent the past few years helping to design control systems for an off-road race car and also driving it, which is either a great way to validate your work or a fast way to learn what you did wrong. The OpenDAQ logger came out of wanting real data instead of just gut feel.

Currently poking at satellite SDR reception because low-earth-orbit weather satellites just broadcast their data openly and I find that delightful. You just need to know where to look. If you want to talk data pipelines, strange hardware, or why your sensor readings look wrong — I'm in.
